Lighthouse Attention: The Training-Time Hierarchy That Makes Quadratic Attention Practical Again - DEV Community

A highly technical, detailed breakdown of a novel attention mechanism optimization with concrete pretraining speedup metrics and architectural details.
  • Symmetric pyramid pooling compresses Q, K, and V together, reducing attention complexity to O(S^2 d) instead of O(NSd).
  • Achieves 1.4-1.7x pretraining wall-clock speedup at 32K-128K context with zero inference overhead.
  • The selection logic lives entirely outside the attention kernel, allowing teams to reuse stock, highly optimized FlashAttention kernels.

[2607.15232v1] In-Place Tokenizer Expansion for Pre-trained LLMs

Presents a highly novel, practical, and detailed recipe for expanding an LLM's tokenizer in-place, solving a major real-world efficiency issue for multilingual deployment.
  • Proposes an in-place tokenizer expansion recipe that continues BPE merges on a multilingual corpus, initializing new token embeddings as the mean of their sub-tokens.
  • Achieves a 2.4x to 4.0x reduction in token count for languages like Hindi, Vietnamese, and Thai.
  • Delivers an estimated 2.2-3.7x per-character decode speedup by combining token reduction with the measured cost of a larger vocabulary.

[2606.14150] Small LLMs: Pruning vs. Training from Scratch

A rigorous academic paper with surprising and practical findings comparing LLM pruning to training from scratch under controlled token budgets.
  • Pruned initialization consistently outperforms random initialization when training token budgets are constrained.
  • The advantage of pruning over training from scratch narrows and nearly vanishes at very high pruning ratios (0.8) or when the training token budget is extremely large.
  • Finer granularity pruning retains a knowledge-transfer advantage that additional training tokens alone cannot recover, whereas coarser structured pruning can be matched by training from scratch.
