Model Routing Is Simple. Until It Isn’t.

Directly addresses ML infra and performance engineering by exposing counter-intuitive systems-level costs in LLM routing.
  • Sticker price is a misleading metric for routing: Claude Sonnet 4.6 was cheaper than GPT-4.1 ($0.19 vs $0.37 per task) due to lower cache-read pricing on highly reused contexts.
  • Task complexity is often invisible at routing time, meaning static heuristics fail when simple prompts trigger complex multi-step agentic loops.
  • Routing must be treated as a multi-dimensional systems optimization problem balancing cost, latency, compliance, and model specialization.
