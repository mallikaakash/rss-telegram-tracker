Introducing Würstchen: Fast Diffusion for Image Generation

Introduces a highly novel architecture achieving 42x spatial compression, directly relevant to efficient model training and inference performance.
  • Würstchen achieves a novel 42x spatial compression using a two-stage decoder (VQGAN + Diffusion Autoencoder), far exceeding the typical 4x-8x compression.
  • Drastically reduces training costs, requiring only 9,000 GPU hours for v1 (512x512) compared to 150,000 hours for Stable Diffusion 1.4.
  • Enables faster inference and lower memory usage, making high-resolution text-to-image generation accessible on consumer-grade GPUs.
  • Leverages performance optimizations like Flash Attention and 'torch.compile' to maximize hardware utilization.

A highly technical deep-dive comparing native quantization schemes (bitsandbytes vs. AutoGPTQ), crucial for LLM inference serving and performance engineering.
