How to pack ternary numbers in 8-bit bytes

Highly technical deep-dive into packing BitNet b1.58 ternary weights (-1, 0, 1) into 8-bit bytes with SIMD-friendly unpacking, directly relevant to LLM inference performance engineering.
  • Packing 5 ternary digits (trits) into an 8-bit byte achieves 1.6 bits per trit, which is 99.06% of the theoretical ideal.
  • Unpacking is optimized for SIMD by replacing slow modulo/division operations with fixed-point multiplication and bit-shifting.
  • The scheme assumes packing is done once (during quantization) while unpacking is done repeatedly during LLM inference, making the unpacking asymmetry highly beneficial.

Introducing Stargate Norway

Details a major 230MW+ AI data center initiative in Europe targeting 100,000 GPUs, which is highly relevant to ML infrastructure and GPU platforms.
  • Stargate Norway is a planned 230MW AI data center (expandable by 290MW) targeting 100,000 NVIDIA GPUs by the end of 2026.
  • The facility will run entirely on renewable hydropower in Narvik and use closed-loop, direct-to-chip liquid cooling.
  • It represents OpenAI's first European infrastructure project under the 'OpenAI for Countries' program, partnering with Nscale and Aker.
