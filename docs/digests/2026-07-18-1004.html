Introducing AutoRound: Intel’s Advanced Quantization for LLMs and VLMs

It details a highly relevant, advanced post-training quantization (PTQ) method that optimizes low-bit LLM/VLM inference, matching the reader's performance engineering focus.
  • AutoRound is a weight-only PTQ method using signed gradient descent to optimize weight rounding and clipping ranges.
  • Outperforms popular baselines at 2-bit precision (INT2), achieving up to 2.1x higher relative accuracy.
  • Supports exporting to GPTQ, AWQ, and GGUF formats, and can quantize a 72B model in 37 minutes on a single A100 GPU.

In-House LLM Serving at Netflix

This is a top-tier engineering deep-dive from Netflix detailing their transition to vLLM, Triton integration, and production trade-offs for LLM serving.
  • Netflix migrated its paved-path LLM serving engine from TensorRT-LLM to vLLM due to better extensibility, debuggability, and support for custom architectures.
  • Integrates vLLM directly into NVIDIA Triton Inference Server within their shared Model Scoring Service (MSS).
  • Details architectural decisions around model packaging, API design, and enforcing output constraints under production load.
