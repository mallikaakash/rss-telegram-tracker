SigLIP 2: A better multilingual vision language encoder

A major vision-language model release from Google detailing significant architectural improvements to the SigLIP encoder.
  • Google releases SigLIP 2, extending the sigmoid loss objective with decoder-based self-distillation, global-local loss, and masked prediction.
  • Introduces a dynamic resolution variant ('naflex') that is highly beneficial for downstream tasks sensitive to aspect ratios.
  • Outperforms original SigLIP across all scales in zero-shot classification, retrieval, and as a vision backbone for VLMs.

Vāgdhenu: A Sanskrit Chanting TTS System

An interesting applied ML project from a top Indian institution (IISc) demonstrating specialized speech synthesis techniques.
  • Uses a flow-matching TTS backbone retrained on a 5-hour single-speaker Sanskrit chant corpus, achieving an expert MOS of 4.6.
  • Routes Sanskrit text through Kannada orthography to prevent Hindi-style schwa-deletion in Devanagari.
  • Employs a vṛtta-aware mechanism to detect poetic meter and select matched references under a half-reference rule.

A new benchmark for evaluating LLM agents on complex, multi-step data analysis tasks, highlighting current performance gaps.
