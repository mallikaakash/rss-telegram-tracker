Fable 5 vs. GPT-5.6 Sol on an NP-Hard Problem: Does /goal help?

It evaluates state-of-the-art reasoning models on a complex, NP-hard operations research optimization problem, directly relevant to applied ML and reasoning capabilities.
  • Fable 5 demonstrated exceptional consistency and intelligence, producing the best overall solution on the KIRO fiber-network design problem.
  • The native `/goal` mode changes the agent's control loop and search path, but it is not a guaranteed 'try harder' switch and can sometimes mature bad ideas.
  • The search space for the Paris network benchmark is massive, with a lower bound of approximately 10^1223 possible valid configurations.
  • The evaluation was conducted using Harbor 0.1.43 and Docker with a 30-minute optimization budget per model run.
Must readOpenAI News

Reasoning models struggle to control their chains of thought, and that’s good

It explores a fundamental safety and alignment property of reasoning models—their inability to deliberately hide or manipulate their internal chain-of-thought.
  • CoT controllability—the ability of a model to deliberately alter its reasoning trace to evade monitoring—is currently low across frontier models.
  • Controllability actually decreases as models are asked to reason for longer periods or undergo additional post-training.
  • This limitation is reassuring for AI safety, as it suggests CoT monitoring remains a highly reliable and interpretable safeguard for now.

Evaluating Audio Reasoning with Big Bench Audio

It introduces a novel benchmark for evaluating audio reasoning and reveals a significant performance gap between native audio and text-based reasoning.
  • Releases 'Big Bench Audio', a 1,000-question dataset adapted from Big Bench Hard to evaluate logical reasoning directly in the audio domain.
  • Reveals a significant 'speech reasoning gap': GPT-4o's accuracy drops from 92% on text to 66% on native Speech-to-Speech.
  • Finds that cascaded Speech-to-Text-to-Text pipelines currently outperform native audio models on complex reasoning tasks.
