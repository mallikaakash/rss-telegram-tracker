Separating signal from noise in coding evaluations

It provides a rigorous technical audit of a major LLM coding benchmark (SWE-Bench Pro), revealing critical flaws that impact how model capabilities are measured.
  • An audit of SWE-Bench Pro reveals that approximately 30% of the tasks are broken or flawed.
  • Identified issues include overly strict tests (27.4% flagged by pipeline), underspecified prompts, low-coverage tests, and misleading prompts.
  • Highlights the difficulty of programmatic benchmark curation and suggests using LLM agents for scalable data quality checks.

Native-speed vLLM transformers modeling backend

This is a major performance engineering update for vLLM, enabling native-speed inference directly from Hugging Face transformers models without custom ports.
  • The transformers modeling backend in vLLM now meets or beats the throughput of hand-written native vLLM implementations.

Introducing Real World VoiceEQ: Measuring the human quality of voice AI

It introduces a new benchmark for evaluating the conversational and emotional quality of voice AI models beyond simple word error rates.
  • Real World VoiceEQ evaluates voice models across 15+ dimensions and 60+ metrics, including tone, emotion, and speaker identity.
  • Built on over 1 million human ratings (785k TTS and 48k STS ratings) using the Kairos evaluation platform.
  • Aims to address the gap where traditional benchmarks overestimate real-world performance by ignoring non-verbal acoustic information.
